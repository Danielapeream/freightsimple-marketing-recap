/01Why this document exists

The foundation is built. The connective layer is what compounds it.

In April 2026 we shipped the foundations: 3 strategic pillars, a 6-stage funnel, ICP and personas, brand book, handbook, customer journey, strategy tree, content plan, retention sequences, and ~30 baseline KPIs. The next layer is the one that connects those foundations to the day-to-day channel work, the place where every new tactic gets placed inside a strategy before anyone starts building it.

This document is that layer. From here forward, every new tactic answers three questions before it ships.

/ Q1
Who is this for?

Carriers, Customers, or Internal team. If the audience isn't named, the tactic has no owner.

/ Q2
Where in the funnel?

Aware → Interested → Qualified → Activated → Converting → Retained. Each stage has its own job.

/ Q3
How do we know it worked?

A specific, named KPI. Not "engagement" or "awareness", a number with a unit.

The rule
If a tactic cannot answer all three questions, it does not get on the roadmap. That is the discipline this document enforces, for us, and for every future hire.
/02North Star

One sentence the whole team can recite.

Marketing exists to grow FreightSimple's marketplace by acquiring, activating, and retaining LTL shippers, while strengthening the supply side that makes the marketplace work, and the culture that makes the company executable.

Three commitments behind the North Star. Every campaign, asset, post, and email must serve at least one of these. If it doesn't, we don't ship it.

/ Commitment 01
Save Money.

Make the economic case visible. Show, don't tell, that FreightSimple shippers pay less than they would through a traditional broker.

/ Commitment 02
Prevent Problems.

Reduce friction at every touchpoint: clearer landing pages, faster quote tool, fewer questions before booking, faster issue resolution after.

/ Commitment 03
Automate Everything.

Marketing operations themselves are a product. Repeatable plays, instrumented funnels, behavior-triggered emails. We scale by building systems, not by adding hands.

/03The Three Pillars

A marketplace has three sides. Marketing serves all three.

FreightSimple is a marketplace. Value flows in two directions, and the company is held together by a third. Each pillar has its own goal, audience, time horizon, and current build state.

Pillar 01 · Supply side
Carriers
// the partners that make the product real
Goal Strong, durable carrier relationships → better rates, more reliability, growth moat.
Audience Canadian LTL carriers, account managers, ops contacts, leadership.
Horizon Mid–long term · relationship-driven, not campaign-driven.
Status ● 0% built · open opportunity
Why it matters Without strong carriers we have no product. Marketing's job here is to make FreightSimple the most pleasant marketplace to partner with.
Pillar 02 · Demand side
Customers
// the revenue engine
Goal Acquire, activate, and retain paying shippers.
Audience Alex (Ops), Sandra (Owner), Michael (Finance), Canadian SMB to mid-market LTL shippers.
Horizon Short–mid term · revenue impact within the quarter.
Status ● ~80% built · execution is the work
Why it matters This is where revenue lives. Most of the channel map sits here, organized by the 6-stage funnel.
Pillar 03 · Culture & alignment
Internal team
// the people who execute everything
Goal Cohesive team, shared vision, strong culture. Marketing builds employer brand.
Audience All FreightSimple employees, current and future hires.
Horizon Ongoing · compounds over months and years.
Status ● Started · handbook live
Why it matters In logistics, talent is scarce. Great internal brand wins recruiting without head hunters, and reduces churn.

/07KPI Tree · how we measure

One North Star. Three pillar metrics. Operational KPIs that ladder up.

Every KPI in this document ladders up to one of three pillar-level metrics, which ladder up to the company's North Star. If a tactic moves a leaf KPI but doesn't ladder up, it's vanity. If a tactic doesn't have a leaf KPI, it doesn't ship.

⭐ North Star Metric
Monthly Bookings
Total bookings generated through the FreightSimple marketplace each month. Every other metric exists to move this one.
Pillar 01 · Carriers
Carrier NPS + active carrier count
Healthy supply enables every demand-side metric. If carriers churn, customers can't get rates.
Pillar 02 · Customers
Funnel conversion rates per stage
North Star (bookings) is the output of how well each stage transitions to the next.
Pillar 03 · Internal
eNPS + handbook adoption + recruiting pipeline
A misaligned team can't execute. Internal health is the leading indicator of external performance.

/08How we work · operating cadences

Clear ownership. No ambiguity about who owns what.

Ambiguity about ownership is the fastest way to stall everything. This section defines how the marketing team divides work, when we sync, and how we measure ourselves. The role labels below are functional, not titles.

Two operating models · pick the one that fits the team's shape

Model A
Strategy + Execution split

Strategy role owns: planning, brand, cross-pillar coordination, leadership communication, KPI dashboard, hiring.

Execution role owns: content production, email setup in Klaviyo, social posting, paid ad management, asset shipping.

Best fit when the team has one strategic role and one execution-shaped role.
Model B
Pillar split

Role 1 owns: Pillars 02 + 03 (Customers + Internal) end-to-end.

Role 2 owns: Pillar 01 (Carriers) end-to-end, plus selected execution support on Pillar 02.

Best fit when both roles are senior enough to own a pillar with its own stakeholders.
How to choose
Pick the model that matches the actual shape of the team today, not the org chart you wish you had. Models can evolve as the team grows. What cannot evolve is the principle: every pillar, every funnel stage, and every channel must have one named owner.

/09Q2 phasing · how the quarter plays out

Foundations first. Then leverage. Then measurement. Then iterate.

The Q2 projects from /06 don't ship in parallel, they ship in a sequence that makes each one possible. The dashboard must be live before we can credibly cut anything; case studies must exist before G2/Trust Pilot have anything to point to; the Explainer Video pairs with the homepage work to actually move the 68%.

Phase 01 · Foundations
Build the floor under everything else
  • Stand up the KPI dashboard (PostHog + Klaviyo + CRM). It multiplies the impact of every other project on the list.
  • Audit Google Ads: identify which keywords convert vs which only spend. Reallocate before scaling.
  • Set up G2 + Trust Pilot listings. Quick foundational win that pays out across the rest of the quarter.
  • Load the retention email sequence into Klaviyo (already written, ready to deploy).
Phase 02 · The 68%
Activate the single biggest lift point in the funnel
  • Kick off the Main Explainer Video, script, vendor or in-house production decision, storyboard.
  • Pair the Explainer Video with a homepage refresh: both target the Interested → Qualified transition.
  • Lock the Help Center revamp scope. Activation friction lives here too.
  • Launch consistent LinkedIn cadence: 3 posts/week across company page + leadership + team voices.
Phase 03 · Proof
Make the dollar math impossible to miss
  • Case study #1: pick the strongest retained customer with the most quantifiable savings story.
  • Case study #2: a second vertical, so prospects in different industries see themselves.
  • Build the review-request flow that triggers after booking #2, feed G2 and Trust Pilot with real signal.
  • First Help Center demo videos shipped, short, product-led, paired with the revamp.
Phase 04 · Read the data
Decide what stays, cuts, and scales
  • Dashboard now has 6+ weeks of baseline data, pull the first real read on funnel-stage transitions.
  • First monthly Pillar deep-dive (start with Customers, the most built pillar).
  • Strategy Base v1.1: incorporate the quarter's learnings. The doc evolves with the team.
  • Quarterly review with leadership: what shipped, what's working, what gets cut from Q3.
The metric that matters most this quarter
Homepage bounce rate. If we move it from 68% to anything below 55% by end of Q2, we will have unlocked the single biggest growth lever in the funnel. Everything else is supporting cast.
