01 · Executive summary

Strategy meets infrastructure.

The execution layer

Six funnel stages — Aware, Interested, Qualified, Activated, Converting, Retained — replace the previous CRM-state model. Each stage has a clear entry criterion, a clear exit criterion, and one priority play. Each play maps to one of the Three Moves in The Strategy.

The email engine sits inside the funnel as the connective tissue. Of the five sequences the funnel needs, one is built and in review (Retention v2.0), three are scoped (Welcome, Qualification nurture, Post-quote nurture), and one needs rebuild (Regular shipper sequence). Without these flows, the platform goes silent at every critical retention moment — which is exactly what's happening today.

The Retention Sequence is the highest-leverage build. It addresses the funnel's biggest gap (no post-first-booking touch), it's already written, and it's where the dollar-first thesis becomes a concrete product. Three emails — Day 2, Day 14, Day 35 — each with real shipment data, real savings math, and the warmly-competent voice the sales calls validated.

03 · The plays per stage

One stage. One priority play.

For every stage: how a company enters, how it exits, and the single play that moves them forward. Each play maps to one of the Three Moves in The Strategy — so every action ladders up to the bigger plan.

Stage 01 · Aware → Move 01 · Translate

Reach the canadian shipper before they're searching.

Pre-CRM stage. The company exists in the world and could benefit from FreightSimple but hasn't interacted with the brand yet. Awareness is driven by content, ads, LinkedIn — not email.

Entry criteria
  • Canadian company shipping LTL freight at least weekly
  • Based in ON, BC, AB, QC, or SK (primary)
  • Ships manufactured, distributed, or imported goods
  • 5–500 employees, not enterprise or retail
Exit criteria → Interested
  • Visits freightsimple.com
  • Clicks a Google or Microsoft ad, or LinkedIn post
  • Signs up for the platform
  • Added to CRM via outbound prospecting
The play · drive awareness through compounding channels

SEO restructure first (240 → 88 pages), then a content engine targeting "LTL freight broker Canada" and adjacent terms. LinkedIn cadence (3–5 posts/week) targeting ops managers and business owners. Microsoft Ads pilot to validate whether the paid-Edge audience mirrors the 24.89% organic conversion. No email — awareness is upstream of the inbox.

Stage 02 · Interested → Move 02 · Automate

Qualify or remove within 48 hours.

A holding stage. Companies should move through it fast — either confirmed as ICP or removed from the active pipeline. The qualification gate is what keeps the funnel clean over time.

Entry criteria
  • Has signed up on freightsimple.com, OR
  • Has been added to CRM by sales team
  • No quote run yet
  • ICP fit not yet confirmed
Exit criteria → Qualified
  • controls_ltl_freight = true confirmed
  • Ships LTL freight at least occasionally
  • Canadian or cross-border shipper
  • Manufacturer / distributor / importer (not broker, retailer, enterprise)
The play · welcome flow + qualification gate

One welcome email immediately on signup. Goal: confirm they ship LTL, explain what FreightSimple does, invite them to run their first quote. Pair with a qualification gate at the data level — before any company joins active pipeline, four checks must pass. Today: companies signing up get silence. The flow does not exist yet.

Stage 03 · Qualified → Move 02 · Automate

First quote within 7 days.

The company has been confirmed as an ICP-fit LTL shipper. They're in the platform but haven't run a quote yet. This is the activation moment.

Entry criteria · all must be true
  • controls_ltl_freight = true
  • Ships LTL freight at least weekly (confirmed)
  • Canada-based or cross-border CA/US
  • Manufacturer, distributor, or importer
Exit criteria → Activated
  • Runs at least 1 quote in the platform
  • OR requests a quote via phone/email
The play · qualification nurture · 2–3 emails over 7 days

Show how easy it is to get a quote. Address the two most common objections — "are your rates competitive?" and "how does it work?" — with concrete proof, not platitudes. Invite to run a first quote with a clear CTA. Existing Klaviyo flow needs rebuild — current version triggers on system state, not journey, so it sends the wrong message at the wrong time.

Stage 04 · Activated → Move 01 · Translate

Turn the quote into a booking within 14 days.

The platform's biggest conversion gap. The "almost there" companies — the ones running 10+ quotes without ever booking — live here. Something specific stops them, and we don't have a flow that addresses it.

Entry criteria
  • Has run at least 1 quote in the platform
  • Has not yet completed a booking
Exit criteria → Converting
  • Completes first booking
  • Shipment confirmed with a carrier
The play · post-quote nurture · address the price-uncertainty objection head-on

The #1 reason people don't book is uncertainty about whether the quote is what they'll actually pay. The flow should explain guaranteed pricing and automatic bill auditing in dollar terms — show the math that makes a first booking zero-risk. Maps to the audit insight: the "almost there" cohort needs a dollar-first reassurance, not a feature list.

Stage 05 · Converting → Move 01 · Translate

From first booking to fifth · the retention motion.

The most critical retention stage. Companies have proven they trust FreightSimple enough to ship with us. Without nurture, most stop here — one and done. This is where Retention Sequence v2.0 lives.

Entry criteria
  • Has completed at least 1 booking
  • Shipment status = Delivered or Completed
  • Total bookings = 1–4
Exit criteria → Retained
  • Completes 5th booking → becomes a Regular customer
The play · Retention Sequence v2.0 · dollar-first across 35 days

Three emails — Day 2, Day 14, Day 35 post-shipment — built around real shipment data and concrete savings math, not generic check-ins. Every email translates the platform's unique capabilities (bill auditing, anomaly detection, guaranteed pricing) into per-shipment dollars. Status: written, in review with the CEO before loading into Klaviyo. Full deep dive in section 05 below.

Stage 06 · Retained → Move 03 · Differentiate

Protect the depth of integration.

Regular customers — 5+ bookings completed, established cadence. The highest-value segment. The retention motion here isn't about getting more bookings — it's about protecting the relationship that already produces them.

Entry criteria
  • Has completed 5+ bookings
  • Active in the last 90 days
  • Established shipping cadence (weekly+)
Exit criteria → At Risk
  • No booking in 90+ days
  • Significant drop in quote volume
  • Direct complaint or unresolved service issue
The play · Regular shipper sequence + relationship layer

Monthly check-in with shipping insights. Quarterly savings summary (how much they saved via bill auditing — actual dollars). Feature updates and new carrier availability. Referral program invitation when ready. Top accounts get human outreach — automation should never replace personal contact at this tier. Existing Klaviyo flow needs full rebuild — current version is generic and segment-blind.

05 · Retention Sequence v2.0

Three emails. Dollar-first across 35 days.

The flagship sequence. Each email shows real shipment data, real savings math, and the warmly-competent voice the sales calls validated. Restrained cadence — 3 emails over 35 days is the right amount for a B2B audience that gets too much email.

Status · v2 proposal pending Natasha review

From "how did it go?" to "here's what you saved."

The v1 sequence asked generic check-in questions. The v2 sequence shows the dollar math. Sales shadowing made it clear: customers see the quote, not the value-add. Every email translates platform capability into per-shipment savings. Tokens like {origin} and {savings} populate from Klaviyo at send.

06 · The 8 principles behind v2.0

The patterns that shaped these emails.

A pattern analysis across 9 reference companies — B2B SaaS leaders, freight and logistics players, and strong-voice consumer brands — distilled into 8 principles. These shape every decision in v2.0 and every flow that comes after.

01

Restrain to earn opens.

Successful B2B emails are less frequent than people fear. 3 emails over 35 days is fine. Don't add more.

02

The subject line is half the battle.

Subject lines should be either clear about value inside, or curiosity-inducing in a way that's honest. Never clickbait.

03

Send from a person, not a brand.

"Daniela from FreightSimple" outperforms "FreightSimple Team" significantly. People reply to people.

04

Use real customer data.

The customer's actual shipments, routes, savings vs. average. Not generic ("we hope you had a great experience"). Specificity equals open rate.

05

Voice lives in microcopy.

The main message stays clear and useful. The personality lives in the subject line, the P.S., the button label, the footer signature.

06

Plain text wins for retention.

A simple, well-written plain text email feels personal. A heavily designed one feels marketing. Email 1 is plain text on purpose.

07

Educate, don't just transact.

The most-opened B2B emails teach something. Even a retention email can include one liner: "did you know we automatically dispute fuel surcharge anomalies?"

08

Forwardable beats personal.

The highest-value B2B email is one the recipient forwards to their boss. Write so that's possible.

Reference companies analyzed
Linear Stripe Notion Flexport Convoy Project44 Mailchimp Cleo Liquid Death

The voice mix for FreightSimple: 80% Stripe (precise, useful, respectful of time) · 15% Linear (calm, restrained, opinionated) · 5% Mailchimp (microcopy with personality). That mix lives in subject lines, button labels, and the occasional P.S. — never in the main body.
