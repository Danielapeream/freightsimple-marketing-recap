01 · Executive summary

The audit in one read.

What the data revealed

FreightSimple does not have a demand problem. The CRM has 400+ companies across four funnel groups, with 95+ active customers shipping regularly and a top-tier segment generating five- and six-figure revenue per account. Demand is healthy.

What the data shows, sharply, is that the leverage points are conversion and retention. The homepage bounces 68% of paid traffic — a fix that compounds across every paid and organic channel. There is no follow-up touch yet at the most critical retention moment — the highest-leverage motion to activate, and a zero-cost win once it ships. SEO is not absent, it's saturated with programmatic content from an earlier strategy that hasn't compounded yet — which means cleanup, not creation, is the first move.

And underneath all of it: two product capabilities — automatic bill auditing and proprietary anomaly detection — that no competitor in the market offers. They are barely mentioned in current messaging. That's the moat. The audit's job was to surface where to point it first.

02 · The 4 funnel groups

Four groups. Four different stories.

The CRM segments into four populations based on where they got stuck. Each one is a different revenue lever — and a different conversation. Sized roughly from the data, exact thresholds in the Funnel Criteria doc.

Group 01 · Regular customers 5+ bookings · ~95 accounts

The ones who stuck.

The ideal customers. They prove the model works.

Profile: high quote-to-shipment ratio (50%+), shipping weekly or more, Canadian-based with frequent cross-border lanes, industrial or manufactured goods, located primarily in ON / BC / AB. Long relationships, often active since 2024–2025. Multiple lanes, multiple shipment types, no contact gaps over 90 days.

Revenue distribution: the top accounts in this group generate disproportionate revenue — five- and six-figure relationships built on platform fit and trust. Protecting and growing this segment is the highest-stakes play in the entire funnel: losing even one of the top accounts has material impact.

What makes them stick · the pattern

Consistent contact with the FreightSimple team, multi-lane usage, integration into ops as the primary LTL solution. None of these are accidental — they're the result of a relationship that gets formed early and reinforced through the platform's reliability. The retention motion isn't "send more emails" — it's protect the depth of integration.

Group 02 · Booked 1–4 times ~90 accounts · 1 most common

The ones who tried us once.

The fastest path to revenue growth — and the biggest current gap.

This group proved they trust FreightSimple enough to complete a transaction. Something stopped them from coming back. The most common booking count is one. After that first shipment, they go quiet — and so do we.

Why they likely didn't come back — multiple hypotheses, mutually compatible: rates were higher than expected on repeat, shipping was a one-time need, they went back to an existing carrier, the experience was platform-fine but human-cold. Most of these are recoverable with the right outreach. None of them are being addressed today.

Critical gap · now identified, sequence in build

There is currently no post-first-booking email sequence. After a customer's first shipment, there's a quiet window in the journey we can fill. A 3-touch nurture sequence at Day 2, Day 14, and Day 35 post-booking has been written (Retention v2.0) and is in review with the CEO before loading into Klaviyo. This is the single zero-cost fix with the highest expected impact in the entire audit. Full mechanic in The Plays →

Group 03 · Quoted, never booked ~100 accounts · split into two sub-groups

The ones who looked and left.

Two sub-populations hidden inside one bucket. Treat them differently.

This group ran quotes but never completed a booking. The temptation is to treat them as one cohort — but they split cleanly into two sub-groups with very different signals.

Just browsing · low intent
  • Most ran 1–3 quotes total
  • Many are individuals (first names only)
  • Platform testing rather than business evaluation
  • Low priority for re-engagement
Almost there · 10+ quotes, no booking
  • Spent significant time in the platform
  • Clear LTL freight need
  • Something specific stopped them
  • High priority for personalized outreach

Hidden noise in this group: several entries are logistics brokers — companies shopping rates to resell, not actual shippers. They should be tagged differently in the CRM and removed from sales follow-up. Cleaning this is a data-quality fix, not a marketing one.

Re-engagement priority

Focus on the high-quote Canadian companies first — the "almost there" segment. Their behavior says they have a real need and the platform almost won them. A targeted outreach explaining guaranteed pricing and bill auditing — the two capabilities the platform never communicated to them — could convert a meaningful subset.

Group 04 · Leads never qualified ~120 accounts · ~30% not ICP at all

The ones who didn't fit.

A qualification gap at the very top of the funnel.

Companies added to the CRM — likely via outbound prospecting in Oct–Nov 2025 — that never ran a quote or showed any intent. Roughly half had zero outreach. About 30% of the list isn't ICP at all: enterprise giants (Tesla-scale companies), grocery chains, government agencies, 3PL/fulfillment centers.

Root cause: outbound prospecting without clear ICP criteria. The list reflects effort, not aim.

The salvageable ~20%: Canadian manufacturers and distributors that match ICP but were never contacted. Worth a re-engagement attempt with proper qualification.

The fix · qualification gate

Before any company is added to active pipeline, four checks: (1) ships LTL freight, (2) Canada-based or cross-border CA/US, (3) ships at least weekly or has clear recurring need, (4) is a manufacturer / distributor / importer — not a broker, retailer, or enterprise. This gate alone reduces noise compounding for years. Full ICP definition in The Customer →

03 · Competitive landscape

Five competitors. Same shape, different threats.

Where FreightSimple actually competes — and where the lines are sharpest. Direct overlap with two LTL platforms in Canada, plus indirect competition from marketplaces, traditional brokers, and TMS software for larger shippers.

Freightera Online marketplace · Canada + US

Their strength Large carrier network, established brand, years of content and SEO authority.

Our edge LTL-only focus (specialists beat generalists), automatic bill auditing, anomaly detection, modern UX.

Freightcom Online broker platform · Canada

Their strength Brand recognition, volume pricing, established Canadian footprint.

Our edge LTL specialization, deeper automation on bill auditing and anomaly detection, more modern platform.

uShip Freight marketplace · US-primary

Their strength Large marketplace, auction pricing, scale.

Our edge Instant guaranteed pricing (no bidding uncertainty), Canadian focus, dedicated LTL specialization.

Traditional brokers Phone / email · Canada

Their strength Relationships, familiarity, the comfort of the known.

Our edge 24/7 self-serve availability, instant quotes, full visibility, no hold times. Modern infrastructure for an industry that defaults to a phone call.

Tailwind / Cargobase TMS software · North America

Their strength Full control for large shippers with dedicated freight teams and existing carrier contracts.

Our edge No setup needed, no carrier contracts, no minimums. A much lower barrier to entry for the segment we actually serve.

The biggest competitive gap to close

Freightera and Freightcom have years of content, SEO, and brand recognition. FreightSimple has a superior product but almost no organic presence. Bill auditing and anomaly detection are completely unique features that no competitor offers — yet they're barely mentioned in current messaging. These two capabilities alone could differentiate FreightSimple in every channel. The audit's biggest single insight is here.

04 · Our unique moats

Two capabilities nobody else has.

Five product features matter to customers. Three of them, competitors have in some form. Two of them are unique to FreightSimple — proprietary, defensible, and currently underused in messaging. They are the entire competitive argument compressed into two sentences.

Unique to us

Automatic bill auditing

Carriers overcharge regularly. Our platform catches and disputes incorrect charges automatically — customers recover money without lifting a finger. Not an add-on. Built into the platform on every shipment.

Unique to us

Anomaly detection

Proactively catches problems — misrouting, delays, customs friction, damage risk — before the customer knows. No competitor offers this level of proactive monitoring. The phrase "see problems coming" is technically defensible because we built the technology that makes it true.

Partial · sharper than competitors

Guaranteed pricing

The price you see is the price you pay. No surprise accessorials, no hidden charges after delivery. Some competitors offer fixed rates; ours is enforced by the bill-auditing system above.

Partial · category-defining

Automatic document generation

BOL and pickup package generated automatically from the booking. No handwritten documents, no dock errors, no faxed forms. A small operational win that compounds across every shipment.

Specialization

LTL-only focus

Competitors are multi-mode (LTL + parcel + FTL). Specialists always beat generalists in customer trust. LTL-only means deeper carrier relationships, sharper rates, and more relevant features. Not a feature — a posture.

Geography

USA + Canada cross-border, one platform

One login, one bill, one experience for cross-border LTL — a real pain for shippers managing dual-carrier setups today. Geographic moat reinforced by the language of "the LTL specialists for Canadian shippers".

07 · The two insights worth their own callout

One play to compound. One signal to act on.

Two findings stand apart from the rest: one is the largest unowned channel — slow to build, asymmetric at scale. The other is the strongest cohort signal in the data, hidden in plain sight until we looked at the browser dimension.

The compounding play

SEO is the largest unowned channel.

Competitors built ranking inventory over years. We have product superiority and audience clarity to start strategically — quality over quantity, intent over volume. A focused content strategy targeting "LTL freight broker Canada," "LTL shipping rates Ontario," and "how to reduce freight costs" can generate compounding inbound traffic at near-zero ongoing cost. The compound rate, not the launch rate, is the point.

The signal worth testing

The Microsoft Edge insight.

Microsoft Edge users convert at 24.89% — roughly 10× the rate of Google Ads campaigns. Edge is the default browser on corporate Windows machines, which is where our ICP works. The cohort signal is structural, not anecdotal. Bing Ads has lower CPCs and far less competition in B2B logistics than Google. A small pilot validates whether the paid-Edge audience mirrors the organic-Edge pattern — high-asymmetry test, low downside.

08 · What we still don't know

Open questions this audit didn't close.

Five threads worth pulling further as more data lands. Each one shapes a future iteration of this document.

01
Awareness layer is invisible We currently can't size the top of the funnel — the population of Canadian shippers exposed to FreightSimple via any channel. PostHog gives us behavior on-site but nothing pre-site. Closing this requires content that compounds plus paid social where the ICP actually is.
02
True churn rate by segment We know the Weekly segment shows ~40% 90-day churn — but the cause split (price, service, product fit) isn't yet documented. Without that split we can't write the right re-engagement message.
03
Quote-to-booking economics The "almost there" sub-group (10+ quotes, 0 bookings) needs structured outreach to learn the actual blocker. Hypothesis: rate sensitivity. Validate before scaling the response.
04
FedEx Freight + Purolator Freight Pages exist for both, but the homepage explicitly says "no parcel/courier." Need to confirm with Ops whether their LTL divisions are active carriers in our network — flagged in the website restructure plan as a blocker.
05
Carrier-side audit This audit is shipper-side. The supply side of the marketplace has not been audited yet — current carrier count, retention, lane coverage, top performers. That's a separate document, scheduled once the shipper-side restructure ships.
