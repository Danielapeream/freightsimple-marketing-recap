01 · Executive summary

The strategy in one read.

The argument

The audit showed us where the leverage lives — conversion and retention. Sales shadowing showed us why — clients aren't loyal to platforms, they're loyal to the lowest quote. They cross-shop us against Freightera, Freightcom, and carrier-direct on every shipment. That changes what retention means. The funnel doesn't end at first booking; it resets with every quote.

From that thesis, three moves follow: translate value into per-shipment dollars across every surface, automate the lifecycle so no account drifts into silence, and differentiate the supply side we curate so price ties have a tiebreaker. These are the three things we do strategically.

Underneath the moves sit three audiences — Internal, Shippers, Carriers — each with its own conversation. And underneath the conversations sits a messaging framework: tagline, voice, persona-specific language, objection responses, and the proof points that make every claim defensible. Every campaign, page, email, and post traces back here.

02 · The thesis · v1 → v2

What changed when we watched sales work.

The original thesis held — but a sales shadowing session on April 23 deepened it into something sharper. Both versions below. The shift is the spine of everything that follows.

v1 · pre-shadowing
We don't have a demand problem. We have a conversion problem.
Audit-derived. The CRM has 400+ companies in the funnel. Demand is healthy. Bounce rate, retention gap, and channel mix are where the leverage lives. True — and the foundation of the Q2 plan. But incomplete.
→
v2 · post-shadowing
Clients aren't loyal to platforms. They're loyal to the lowest quote. We compete for every shipment — not just the first one.
Sales confirmed what funnel data only whispered: customers split shipments across platforms based on price-per-shipment. Each quote is a fresh competition. Retention isn't "get them to book twice" — it's "earn every single shipment, every time." The funnel doesn't end at first-booking. It resets with every quote request.

What this changes: messaging stops talking about features and starts talking dollar math. Retention emails stop sounding like a check-in and start sounding like a quote-comparison tool. Site copy stops listing capabilities and starts answering "what does this save you on this shipment?"

08 · Voice & tone

How we sound.

Voice is consistent across every channel. Tone shifts by context — more empathetic in support, more direct in sales, more playful on LinkedIn. The voice itself doesn't change.

Voice attributes

Confident, not arrogant.

Our tech is genuinely different (anomaly detection is unique in the market). We say it clearly without bragging. We let the product and customer proof do the talking.

Human, not corporate.

We use contractions. We write like we talk. We skip the jargon — pipeline optimization, synergistic solutions, best-in-class — because real people don't talk like that.

Direct, not verbose.

Say what you mean in as few words as possible. "Zero surprises" beats "we eliminate unexpected issues through proactive monitoring" every time.

Slightly irreverent.

Freight takes itself too seriously. We don't. "No drama," "enough is enough," "it doesn't have to be this hard" — professional but not stiff.

Words we use vs. words we don't

Do say
  • "Zero surprises"
  • "Smarter LTL"
  • "Instant quotes"
  • "In minutes, not weeks"
  • "Guaranteed pricing"
  • "Anomaly detection"
  • "No drama"
  • "100+ trusted carriers"
  • "Catch problems early"
  • "Real customer stories"
Don't say
  • "Synergy / synergistic"
  • "Best-in-class"
  • "Revolutionary"
  • "Cutting-edge"
  • "Solution provider"
  • "Pipeline optimization"
  • "Leverage our platform"
  • "World-class"
  • "Seamless experience"
  • "Next-generation"

Tone shift by channel: website and emails are confident + helpful. LinkedIn is personable + opinionated. Ads are direct + benefit-focused. Sales decks are confident + data-driven. Customer support is empathetic + solution-oriented. The voice stays the same; the tone adjusts.

11 · Objection handling

The five concerns and how we answer them.

The top concerns prospects raise — in sales calls, demo requests, and reply emails. Each one with a quick response (1–2 sentences for fast replies) and a full response (for nuanced conversations). Pick the one that fits the context.

Objection 01
We already have a freight broker. Why switch?
Quick response

Because switching to FreightSimple isn't really switching — it's upgrading. No contract, no minimums, no risk. Try one shipment and compare.

Full response

Most brokers are built for a world that doesn't exist anymore — phone calls, paper forms, reactive problem-solving. FreightSimple gives you instant quotes, guaranteed pricing, electronic booking, automatic bill auditing, and anomaly detection technology no other broker has. There's no contract required and no minimum volume, so you don't have to switch all your freight — start with one shipment, compare the experience, and scale from there at your own pace.

Objection 02
Isn't going direct to carriers cheaper?
Quick response

It can be — until something goes wrong. We offer competitive rates from 100+ carriers plus a safety net you don't get going direct.

Full response

Carrier-direct works for companies with dedicated freight staff and consistent volume. For everyone else, the hidden costs add up fast: time spent calling carriers for quotes, disputing incorrect bills, managing damaged freight, and scrambling when a carrier has no availability on your lane. FreightSimple gives you instant access to 100+ carriers at competitive rates, automatic bill auditing, and a platform that handles the logistics so you can focus on your business. Going direct trades upfront savings for ongoing operational overhead — we remove both.

Objection 03
Our freight volume is too low for this to matter.
Quick response

Actually, low volume is exactly who FreightSimple is built for. No minimums, no contracts, instant access to bulk pricing without bulk commitments.

Full response

Traditional brokers require volume commitments to give you competitive rates, which forces low-volume shippers to either overpay or overcommit. FreightSimple gives every customer access to negotiated carrier rates from day one, with no minimums and no contracts. You get bulk buying power without bulk obligations. Plus, the time savings from instant quotes and electronic booking matter more when you're a small team — you can't afford to spend hours on freight logistics when you're wearing five hats.

Objection 04
How is anomaly detection actually different from tracking?
Quick response

Tracking tells you where your shipment is. Anomaly detection tells you when something's wrong — before you'd otherwise notice.

Full response

Traditional tracking shows you the scan history: picked up, in transit, delivered. That's useful, but reactive — by the time you see "exception" in the scan, the problem is usually real and expensive. Anomaly detection is proactive: our system monitors shipment patterns and proactively flags anything that looks off — unexpected delays, routing that suggests misrouting, timing that suggests a missed connection, destination mismatches. You get alerts early enough to intervene. It's the difference between finding out about a problem from your customer or finding out before your customer does.

Objection 05
Setting up new software is a nightmare. How long until we're live?
Quick response

You can book your first shipment the same day you sign up. No credit applications, no lengthy onboarding, no consultants.

Full response

We built FreightSimple specifically to avoid the painful onboarding typical of logistics software. There's no credit application process (you pay per shipment). No minimum commitment (cancel anytime, though nobody does). No training required (if you can book a hotel online, you can book a shipment). Most customers sign up, get a quote, and book their first shipment within 30 minutes. For larger companies with integration needs, we have pre-built connectors for NetSuite, Shopify, QuickBooks, and a full API — but none of that is required to start.
