/03The headline finding
★ What explains a lot of what we've been seeing

The account is optimizing for signups, not for first bookings.

Smart Bidding is learning from the 18 signup events, not from the 3 first booking events. That means every algorithmic decision (which auctions to bid on, which audiences to favor, which placements to push) is tuned to a proxy of revenue, not to revenue itself. Before reallocating budget or changing bid strategies, the configuration needs to be aligned with what we actually want to grow.

↳ What this means in plain terms
Of every 100 clicks paid for, about 1 becomes a signup, and about 1 in 6 signups becomes a first booking. If the account were optimized for first bookings, the algorithm would be making different bid decisions, possibly favoring keywords and audiences that signal serious buying intent over those that simply convert to signup. This is the highest-leverage adjustment for Phase 01 of the Paid Media Plan, and it can be made before any budget change.

/07What we're adjusting

Three weeks, in sequence. Configuration first. Then read the data. Then move budget.

The Paid Media Plan structures the next 90 days into Audit · Reallocate · Scale. The first three weeks (Audit) line up with what this report surfaces. Each step depends on the previous one. Skipping ahead, especially moving budget before the configuration is corrected, would mean reallocating based on a signal that's about to change.

★ This week
Configuration fixes
  • Promote First Booking from secondary to primary objective
  • Resolve the "Compra" misconfiguration alert
  • Investigate the Google Tag warning (estimated +1.7% lift)
  • Resolve the conflicting negative keyword in both campaigns
Week 2
Bid strategy assessment
  • Once Smart Bidding is learning from bookings, evaluate Maximize Conversions vs current Maximize Clicks
  • CA has 15 signups/month, near the threshold for Smart Bidding stability
  • US needs more conversion volume before any Smart Bidding switch
  • Pull search term reports to identify negative keyword candidates
Week 3
Reallocation read
  • Cross-reference CRM: which of the 3 first bookings are US vs CA
  • If CA confirms efficiency at booking level, propose budget shift toward CA
  • Pause or restructure ad groups with zero signups over 30 days
  • Document baseline cost-per-first-booking by campaign for Phase 02 comparison
↳ Why sequence matters
If we shift budget from US to CA today, we'd be doing it based on signup data from a misconfigured optimization. Three weeks of sequenced cleanup gives the next budget decision a clean baseline to stand on. The total spend in the meantime stays at the current ~$300/day cap, no extra commitment needed to do this work.
/08Open questions · data gaps

What we don't know yet, and what closes each gap.

Honest list. Reporting what we know is half the work. Surfacing what we still need to know is the other half, because those are the questions that shape the next monthly update.

Where are the 3 first bookings coming from?
US or CA? The 5× signup gap might or might not hold at the booking level. This is the single most important question to resolve before any budget reallocation. Closes with: CRM cross-reference, Week 3 of the cleanup sequence.
Why is the "Compra" conversion misconfigured?
The account shows a "Compra" conversion action with status "configuration error" and 0 campaigns associated. Likely this should be the First Booking event, mapped to the right campaigns. Resolving this may surface bookings that aren't currently being attributed. Closes with: Google Ads UI inspection this week.
What's the signup → first booking conversion rate by campaign?
Currently 18 signups produce 3 bookings (16.7% overall). We don't know if that rate is the same for US and CA, or if one geography has a meaningfully better signup quality. Closes with: CRM data join, Week 2-3.
Is the Quality Score issue on "day and ross freight" and "abf freight" a landing page mismatch or an ad relevance issue?
Both keywords show "Eligible (limited)". The fix differs depending on whether the issue is the landing page (carrier name prominence) or the ad copy (carrier relevance). Closes with: landing page audit, Week 1.
Are Search Partners contributing or just spending?
Search Partners is enabled in both campaigns but performance can't be segmented from Google Search itself in the current view. This is typical when Search Partners gets disabled by default during account audits, worth checking the conversion contribution. Closes with: segmented report pull, Week 2.
Should the US campaign target be widened beyond carrier-named search?
US generic terms ("us freight shipping", "freight company usa") have clicks but zero signups, while CA generic terms convert. The hypothesis is landing page mismatch (the US homepage may not orient to a US-visiting prospect well). Closes with: landing experience review + landing page test, Phase 02 (Week 4-6).
