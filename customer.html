01 · Executive summary

The customer in one read.

Who we serve

Our customer is a small-to-mid-sized Canadian manufacturer, distributor, or importer shipping LTL freight at least weekly — often multiple times per week. They are based primarily in Ontario or BC, with growing presence in Alberta and Quebec. Their goods are physical, industrial, and often cross-border. Shipping is mission-critical to their business but isn't their core competency.

Inside every account live three buyer personas: Alex the Operations Manager (the primary user, books daily), Sandra the Business Owner (the decision-maker, cares about reputation), and Michael the Finance Controller (the budget approver, cares about predictability). The same FreightSimple capabilities matter to all three — but the language, the emphasis, and the proof points are different per role.

Underneath the cohort: they're time-starved, price-sensitive on a per-shipment basis, and they cross-shop us against Freightera and carrier-direct on every quote. Sales shadowing made this concrete in ways CRM data alone couldn't surface — and it's the layer that reshapes the messaging across every channel.

04 · Persona deep dive
OM · Operations Manager Mid-sized manufacturer or distributor · ON or BC · 5–15 yrs experience

Alex · the daily operator.

Alex books shipments every day. He becomes a hero when ops runs quietly. He becomes a firefighter when it doesn't.

Pain points
  • Wastes hours every week quoting via phone and email
  • Handwritten BOLs cause errors at the dock
  • Hard to track shipments across multiple carriers
  • Finds out about problems too late to prevent them
  • Carrier account reps change constantly
Goals
  • Get quotes instantly without picking up the phone
  • Book pickups electronically — no mix-ups
  • Know where every shipment is at all times
  • Catch problems before they escalate
  • Spend less time on freight admin
Triggers
  • A shipment gets lost or seriously delayed
  • Their carrier rep leaves and service drops
  • Boss asks why freight costs are unpredictable
  • Team grows and the manual process can't keep up
Objections
  • "We already have a carrier we work with"
  • "Setup takes too long, we don't have time"
  • "I don't want to learn a new system"
  • "What if rates are higher than what we have?"
How to talk to Alex

Lead with time savings and reliability. Show the platform in action — instant quotes, electronic booking, automatic tracking. The anomaly detection feature is a huge differentiator: "we catch problems before you even know they exist." Instant setup (minutes, not weeks) is also key — Alex doesn't have time for an onboarding marathon.

05 · Persona deep dive
BO · Business Owner Small manufacturer or importer · 10–100 employees · Wears many hats

Sandra · the buck-stops-here decision-maker.

Sandra wears five hats. Shipping shouldn't be one of them — but when freight goes wrong, the customer call lands on her desk.

Pain points
  • Freight costs feel unpredictable and hard to control
  • Too much of her time spent managing shipping issues personally
  • Surprised by extra charges on freight bills
  • Can't easily see what shipping is costing the business
  • Customers complain when deliveries are late
Goals
  • Reduce total freight spend without sacrificing service
  • Free up time — let someone else handle shipping
  • No surprise charges or hidden fees
  • Scale shipping operations as the business grows
  • Keep customers happy with on-time deliveries
Triggers
  • A big surprise charge on a freight bill
  • A customer lost due to a late shipment
  • Referral from another business owner she trusts
  • Business is growing and current process can't scale
Objections
  • "Is FreightSimple actually cheaper than what we use?"
  • "We're too small — is this for companies like us?"
  • "I don't want to sign a long-term contract"
  • "We've had bad experiences with brokers before"
How to talk to Sandra

Lead with total cost savings and peace of mind. Guaranteed pricing, automatic bill auditing, and real humans who answer the phone are huge for Sandra. She wants to hand off the headache — position FreightSimple as an extension of her team. Case studies and referrals from similar companies will close her faster than any feature list.

06 · Persona deep dive
FA · Finance / Controller Mid-sized company · Reports to owner or CFO · Controls the purse strings

Michael · the reconciliation and predictability gatekeeper.

Michael spends part of every month disputing charges that shouldn't be there in the first place. The bills he gets are opaque. He just wants the math to be simple.

Pain points
  • Freight bills are opaque and hard to reconcile
  • Incorrect charges go unnoticed and cost real money
  • No easy way to report on freight spend by period
  • Unpredictable costs make budgeting very difficult
  • Payment terms don't always match cash flow needs
Goals
  • Predictable, guaranteed freight costs every month
  • Never pay an incorrect charge again
  • Clean reporting to track freight spend over time
  • Flexible payment terms that match cash flow
  • One invoice, one place — not chasing multiple carriers
Triggers
  • Discovers they've been overcharged repeatedly
  • Ops team requests a better freight solution
  • Year-end audit reveals messy freight accounting
  • CFO asks for a freight cost reduction plan
Objections
  • "Can you integrate with our accounting system?"
  • "What are the payment terms?"
  • "How do we know we're actually saving money?"
  • "Who handles billing disputes if something goes wrong?"
How to talk to Michael

Lead with guaranteed pricing, automatic bill auditing, and clean reporting. The fact that FreightSimple handles all AP and challenges incorrect charges automatically is a massive selling point. Show the Insights feature — graphs, Excel reports, spend by period. Mention credit terms for larger accounts and flexible payment options.

08 · What sales taught us

The shadowing layer. Six insights that changed everything.

A shadowing session with the sales team on April 23 surfaced things the CRM data couldn't. Six insights below — each one with the actual quote from the conversation and what it means for marketing. This is the layer that reshaped the strategic thesis.

Decision driver ● High impact

Price is the #1 decision factor — every time.

Clients split shipments across platforms based on who offers the best price for that specific shipment. Freightera one day, us the next. They rarely see the value-add — they see the quote.
What this means Value-add features (bill auditing, anomaly detection, 100+ carriers) aren't being translated to dollars. We need to reframe messaging — not "what we do," but what it saves you per shipment. This insight became Move 01 in The Strategy →
Audience profile Confirms ICP

Decision-makers are corporate, time-starved, serious.

They speak fast. They're serious. They value human contact but want it concise. No fluff. No long sales conversations. Quick, trust-building, done.
What this means Validates the Microsoft Edge signal — 24.89% conversion is a corporate fingerprint. Copy across the site must be tight, substantive, no fluff. Emails must be short. Long-form content gets skipped.
Automation gap ● Blocks scale

Everything runs on manual CRM templates.

There's no automated email flow. Follow-ups, surveys, re-engagements — all sent manually from the CRM, template by template. Which means most accounts haven't received one yet. Hundreds of quoted clients waiting for the right next touch.
What this means The Retention Sequence becomes critical path, not "nice to have." Klaviyo setup is the highest-leverage ops move available. One person in sales cannot manually nurture hundreds of accounts.
Relationship priority ● Strategic core

Strengthening relationships is the #1 internal priority.

Large, frequent shippers are the focus — as they should be. But there's a long tail of mid-volume accounts silently drifting. With one person handling all of sales, most don't get real attention. We need automation to cover them.
What this means Beyond retention, we need a dormant account reactivation play (already in Sprint 02) plus ongoing low-touch nurture. Goal: every account feels seen, even the small ones.
Supply-side opportunity Marketing lever

No carrier scoring exists — but it should.

We've considered carrier scoring and decided it wouldn't add value. Marketing sees it differently — a scoring system would let us curate the carrier network, offer better placement to top performers, and build a defensible story.
What this means Add Carrier Scoring to Q3 pipeline as a cross-functional initiative (Ops + Marketing). Turns an operational metric into a marketplace differentiator and a marketing narrative — "our carriers average 97% on-time". Mapped to Move 03 in The Strategy →
Positioning clarity Message fix

Clients want parcel + freight. We only do LTL.

Prospects ask about parcel all the time. It's a completely different business. We need to be explicit and unambiguous: FreightSimple is LTL-only — not because we can't, but because specialization is the value.
What this means Homepage and nav need to clarify what we do and don't do, upfront. Turn the limitation into a positioning win — "the LTL specialists for Canadian shippers." Become the category authority.
